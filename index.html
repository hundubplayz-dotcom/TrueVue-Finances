About This Project

The Story Behind the Data

TrueVue is a virtual company I co-founded and managed during my senior year of high school through Virtual Enterprise International (VEI) — a program where students create and operate simulated businesses in a real competitive marketplace alongside thousands of firms worldwide.

Our product, the TrueVue RoadLock, addresses a real public safety crisis: 250 teenagers die in car accidents every month in the US, and teen drivers use their phones nearly 25% of the time they're behind the wheel — increasing crash risk by 23x. RoadLock physically locks the phone while transforming it into a high-quality dashcam, cutting distraction and post-accident costs simultaneously.

Over seven months, our 30-person team secured a B2B contract with Best Buy worth $136,172/month (50% profit margin), ran 5 trade shows across Southern California, and launched an e-commerce channel — generating over $1,043,759 in actual revenue through March 31, 2026.

Now a college freshman, I built this dashboard to merge my interests in business analysis and frontend development — applying authentic financial records to a data visualization project built entirely from scratch with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

All figures are authentic records from TrueVue's accounting master document. April 2026 data is projected per the approved budget. TrueVue is structured as an S-Corporation, enabling pass-through taxation and protecting shareholders from personal liability — advantageous for a medium-to-high liability product like a driver-safety device.
